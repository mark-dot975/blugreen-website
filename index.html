Biochar: The Carbon Sponge

That Feeds Life in Soil

Turning waste biomass into long-term carbon and microbial habitat

Biochar is produced through pyrolysis of biomass at 350-700°C with little oxygen, creating a stable carbon structure that can remain in soil for hundreds of years.

Carbon Sequestration

Locks CO₂ into soil for long-term storage
